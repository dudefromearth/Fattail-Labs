### Lesson 1 — `stop-the-bleeding-mindset`

**title:** The stop-the-bleeding mindset  

**outcomes:**
- State why process comes before optimization
- Write one personal stop-the-bleeding rule (observable, no profit target)

**on_screen:**
- Stop the bleeding first
- Process before optimization
- Your one rule this week

**script:**  
Capital preservation is the first step to trading success — and for many people, the
only step they need for a long time. We are not here to sell a dream of easy money.
We are here to stop the bleeding: the pattern of avoidable damage that comes from
acting without a process.

Bleeding versus thriving is a sequence, not a personality. You do not fix a leaking
hull by polishing the sails. First you stop the water. In trading terms: define what
“damage” means for you, and put a rule in front of it before you chase upside.

Risk language comes before reward language. If you cannot say what would make you
stand down, you are not ready to talk about opportunity. That is not fear — that is
professional order of operations.

One habit for this week: review adherence and drawdown behavior, not a highlight reel
of wins. Ask: Did I follow the process I claimed? Where did I improvise?

Close by writing one sentence: your personal stop-the-bleeding rule. Keep it
observable. No profit targets in that sentence — process only.

### Lesson 2 — `define-risk-before-reward`

**title:** Define risk before reward  

**outcomes:**
- Name max-loss framing and invalidation
- List stand-down criteria in plain language

**on_screen:**
- Define risk first
- Invalidation
- Stand down

**script:**  
Today we define risk in plain language — for one structure or strategy, not for the
entire market. Risk is not a vibe. It is the set of conditions under which you accept
loss, and the conditions under which you refuse to continue.

Three pieces: max-loss framing (what is the most you are willing to be wrong in this
setup), invalidation (what observation means the idea is done), and stand-down
criteria (what personal or market state means you do not take the next trade even if
the setup “looks good”).

Misconceptions: risk is only position size; risk is only feeling scared; risk is
something you feel after the loss. We flip that: risk is designed before entry.

You will leave with a short checklist you can reuse: max loss defined, invalidation
named, stand-down named, process note written. No income claims. Capacity over
dependency: this checklist is yours to keep.

### Lesson 3 — `weekly-process-review`

**title:** Weekly process review  

**outcomes:**
- Complete a one-page weekly process review
- Choose one focus for next week without a profit scoreboard

**on_screen:**
- Weekly process review
- Adherence · Exceptions · One focus

**script:**  
A weekly process review is how you turn trading into a craft instead of a mood. This
is not a scoreboard of profits. It is a review of adherence, exceptions, and one
adjustment.

Template: rules followed; rules broken; emotional triggers; next week’s single focus.
If you broke a rule and made money, that still counts as a process miss — luck is not
a curriculum. If you followed the process and lost, that is data, not a moral failure.

Keep the review to one page. Choose one focus for next week — only one. Capacity grows
from repeated honest review, not from collecting gurus.

Close: schedule the review like a standing meeting with yourself. Process outcomes only.
