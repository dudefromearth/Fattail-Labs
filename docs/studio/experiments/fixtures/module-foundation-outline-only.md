# Format α — Module outline only (Foundation)

Use for HeyGen delivery-format experiment P1.  
No full VO — teaching briefs only.

---

## MODULE: Foundation

**description_md:**  
Why capital preservation and process come before optimization. Sets the frame for
the rest of the course. Learners should leave able to talk about risk and process
without profit theater.

---

### Lesson 1 — `stop-the-bleeding-mindset`

**title:** The stop-the-bleeding mindset  
**outcomes:**
- State why process comes before optimization
- Define a personal “stop the bleeding” rule in one sentence

**teaching_brief (~120 words):**  
Teach capital preservation as the first step. Bleeding vs thriving is a sequence,
not a personality type. Risk is defined before reward language. Offer one weekly
habit: review drawdown and rule adherence, not P&L bragging. Tone calm and adult.
No income promises. Close with: write one sentence defining your personal stop-the-bleeding rule.

---

### Lesson 2 — `define-risk-before-reward`

**title:** Define risk before reward  
**outcomes:**
- Name max-loss framing and invalidation in plain language
- List what would make them stand down

**teaching_brief (~130 words):**  
Define risk for a single structure or strategy. Cover max loss framing, invalidation,
and stand-down criteria. Correct misconceptions: risk as only “feeling scared,” risk
as only position size. Leave a short reusable checklist. No income language.
Tone instructional and checklist-friendly.

---

### Lesson 3 — `weekly-process-review`

**title:** Weekly process review  
**outcomes:**
- Complete a one-page weekly process review
- Choose one focus for next week without a profit scoreboard

**teaching_brief (~110 words):**  
Run a weekly process review: adherence, exceptions, one adjustment. Template fields:
rules followed, rules broken, emotional triggers, next week’s single focus. Explicitly
not a P&L brag sheet. Tone coaching but dignified.
