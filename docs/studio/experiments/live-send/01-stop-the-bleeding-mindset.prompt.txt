SCRIPT (concept and theme to convey — not a verbatim transcript. Expand naturally, add plain examples, do not pad with silence):

Capital preservation is the first step to trading success — and for many people, the only step they need for a long time. We are not here to sell a dream of easy money. We are here to stop the bleeding: the pattern of avoidable damage that comes from acting without a process.

Bleeding versus thriving is a sequence, not a personality. You do not fix a leaking hull by polishing the sails. First you stop the water. In trading terms: define what "damage" means for you, and put a rule in front of it before you chase upside.

Risk language comes before reward language. If you cannot say what would make you stand down, you are not ready to talk about opportunity. That is not fear — that is professional order of operations.

One habit for this week: review adherence and drawdown behavior, not a highlight reel of wins. Ask: Did I follow the process I claimed? Where did I improvise?

Close by writing one sentence: your personal stop-the-bleeding rule. Keep it observable. No profit targets in that sentence — process only.
