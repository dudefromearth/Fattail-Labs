SCRIPT (concept and theme to convey — not a verbatim transcript. Expand naturally, do not pad with silence):

Today we define risk in plain language — for one structure or strategy, not for the entire market. Risk is not a vibe. It is the set of conditions under which you accept loss, and the conditions under which you refuse to continue.

Three pieces: max-loss framing (what is the most you are willing to be wrong in this setup), invalidation (what observation means the idea is done), and stand-down criteria (what personal or market state means you do not take the next trade even if the setup "looks good").

Misconceptions: risk is only position size; risk is only feeling scared; risk is something you feel after the loss. We flip that: risk is designed before entry.

You will leave with a short checklist you can reuse: max loss defined, invalidation named, stand-down named, process note written. No income claims. Capacity over dependency: this checklist is yours to keep, not a signal feed to rent forever.
