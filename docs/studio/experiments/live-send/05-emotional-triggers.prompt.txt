LESSON TITLE: Map your emotional triggers
OUTCOMES:
- Name three personal triggers that pull you off process
- Pair each with a pre-committed process response

SCRIPT (concept and theme to convey — expand naturally, no silence padding):

Process fails most often at the emotion–action gap: you know the rule, then FOMO, revenge, boredom, or relief after a win writes a new rule in the moment. Today we map triggers without self-hatred.

A trigger is a state or event that reliably precedes process breaks for you. Examples: after two losses; when a friend posts a win; late session fatigue; news spike. Your list is personal — steal structure, not someone else's biography.

For each trigger, write a response that is process, not pep talk: stand down for N minutes; checklist only; reduce size to zero new risk; end the session. The response must be executable when you are least wise.

This is capacity: you are building a map you own. It is not a guru telling you how you feel. No income language. No "channel the emotion into a bigger trade."

Close: three triggers, three responses, written down. Tomorrow you will pick one focus for a full week so the map gets practice, not shelfware.
