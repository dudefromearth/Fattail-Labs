LESSON TITLE: One-focus week
OUTCOMES:
- Choose one process focus for seven days
- Define what "done" looks like without a P&L scoreboard

SCRIPT (concept and theme to convey — expand naturally, no silence padding):

Improvement dies when everything is a priority. This lesson is one rule: one focus for seven days. Not five goals. Not a new personality. One observable process focus.

Examples of good focuses: run the checklist before every decision; end session at a fixed clock time; write the weekly review on the same day; zero trades when stand-down criteria fire. Bad focuses: "make money," "be more disciplined" with no behavior attached, "win rate up."

Define done without profit: "I ran the checklist on every discretionary decision," or "I completed one one-page review." If you miss days, the focus is still the focus — count adherence, not drama.

Tie back to stop-the-bleeding: one focus reduces thrashing, which reduces avoidable damage. Capacity over dependency: you choose the focus; no one sells it to you weekly.

Close the course arc: mindset, risk definition, review, checklist, triggers, one focus. Invite them to schedule the next weekly review and keep the tools. Process outcomes only.
