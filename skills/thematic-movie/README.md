## What a thematic movie is

A **long-form cinematic doctrine essay** (target 8–20 min): one theme from the
FatTail doctrine — *why accounts die*, *the unbounded loser*, *convexity as a
discipline* — told as a narrative film rather than a talking-head lesson.
Three-act structure, cinematic avatar delivery, b-roll and motion graphics,
scored. It teaches by story; the course library teaches by curriculum. The
movie is top-of-funnel: public on YouTube, feeding the pathway.

**Not** a lesson, not a coaching short, not a campaign asset — though its
derivative clips may serve campaigns.

## Pack invariants

1. **Never silent-publish.** The movie and every derivative clip land as
   proposals; YouTube upload is a human act until doctrine says otherwise.
2. **Hotel gates every claim** (trading accuracy), **Tango gates the framing**
   (honest marketing, capacity-over-dependency) — both before
   `awaiting_approval`.
3. **No performance porn.** No P&L flexing, no equity curves as proof, no
   implied returns — the doctrine sells survival, not riches.
4. **Cast from the registry only** (`docs/studio/cast/`); never invent
   presenters. Music/b-roll must be licensed (HeyGen library or owned assets).
5. Derivative shorts (AI Clipping) are **children of the movie card**, not
   free-floating content.
